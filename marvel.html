MARVEL — Monitoring, Assessment, Reporting, Verification, Evaluation and Learning — is the results-and-learning system of the Global Fire Management Hub.

Developed by Marcelo Rezende under the FAO–GEF Drylands Sustainable Landscapes Impact Programme (DSL-IP), MARVEL is applied here to the Canada-funded Integrated Fire Management work (GCP/GLO/1351/CAN). It turns the project's activity into evidence, accountability and improvement — joining the results framework, indicators, activities and learning in one living view.

A modular system

MARVEL core functions

MARVEL is built from six modular core functions — Monitoring, Assessment, Reporting, Verification, Evaluation and Learning. Each is designed to be assembled and customised to a country's specific context, so that evidence-based, informed decision-making can take place. Together they join MRV — the measurement and assurance of results — with MEL — the learning that feeds lessons back into delivery.

MRV — Monitoring, Reporting & Verification

The accountability spine: measuring environmental and social results, reporting them against agreed targets, and verifying that the data and benefits hold up.

MEL — Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning

The improvement loop: evaluating outcomes and feeding lessons back into delivery so the next cycle of Integrated Fire Management is better than the last.
